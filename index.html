Freo D'Octa Arrives

Text and translations


  1. Jan Pieterszoon Sweelinck (1562-1621) - A Dieu ma voix (Psalm 77)

    Pseaume 77

    A Dieu ma voix j'ay haussée,
    Et ma clameur addressée.
    A Dieu ma voix a monté,
    Et mon Dieu m'a escouté.
    Au jour de ma grand' destresse,
    Dieu a esté mon addresse,
    Et du soir au lendemain
    Je luy ay tendu la main.

    Psalm 77

    To God, I raised my voice,
    And I directed my outcry to Him.
    To God, my voice has ascended,
    And my God has listened to me.
    On the day of my great distress,
    God was my recourse,
    And from evening to morning
    I reached out my hand to Him.

  2. Alonso Lobo (1555-1617) - Versa est in luctum

    Versa est in luctum cithara mea,
    et organum meum in vocem flentium.
    Parce mihi Domine,
    nihil enim sunt dies mei.

    My harp is turned to grieving
    and my flute to the voice of those who weep.
    Spare me, oh Lord,
    for my days are as nothing.

  3. Tomas Luis de Victoria (1548-1611) - O magnum mysterium

    O magnum mysterium
    et admirabile sacramentum,
    ut animalia viderent Dominum natum
    jacentem in praesepio.
    O beata Virgo, cujus viscera meruerunt
    portare Dominum Jesum Christum.
    Alleluia!

    O great mystery
    and wonderful sacrament,
    that animals should see the new-born Lord
    lying in a manger!
    O blessed is the Virgin, whose womb
    was worthy to bear Christ the Lord.
    Hallelujah!

  4. Francis Poulenc (1899-1963) - O magnum mysterium

    as above

  5. Alonso Lobo (1555-1617) - Vivo ego, dicit Dominus

    Vivo ego, dicit Dominus,
    nolo mortem peccatoris
    sed ut magis convertatur
    et Vivat.

    I live, says the Lord,
    and do not desire the death of the sinner,
    but rather that he change his ways
    and live.

  6. Johan Sebastian Bach (1685-1750) - Buss und Reu (St Matthew Passion)

    Buß und Reu
    Knirscht das Sündenherz entzwei
    Dass die Tropfen meiner Zähren
    Angenehme Spezerei
    Treuer Jesu, dir gebären

    Woe and rue
    tear my sinful heart in two
    Teardrops falling serve to show
    Thee, Jesus once again
    what a mighty debt I owe Thee.

  7. Johan Sebastian Bach (1685-1750) - Den Tod niemand zwingen kunnt

    Den Tod niemand zwingen kunnt
    Bei allen Menschenkindern
    Das macht’ alles unsre Sünd,
    Davon kam der Tod so bald
    Und nahm über uns Gewalt,
    Hielt uns in seinem Reich gefangen.
    Und alls zu nichte macht
    Alleluja!

    Nobody could restrain death,
    Among all the children of humankind;
    Our sin made all that so;
    No innocence was to be found.
    As a result, death came immediately
    And seized power over us,
    Held us imprisoned in its realm.
    Hallelujah.

  8. Felix Mendelssohn (1809-1847) - Hebe deine Augen auf (Elias)

    Hebe Deine Augen auf zu den Bergen,
    von welchen dir Hilfe kommt.
    Deine Hilfe kommt vom Herrn,
    der Himmerl und Erde gemacht hat.
    Er wird deinen Fuss nicht gleiten lassen.
    Und der dich behuete schlaeft nicht

    Lift thine eyes, O lift thine eyes to the mountains, whence cometh, help.
    Thy help cometh from the Lord,
    the Maker of heaven and earth.
    He hath said, “Thy foot shall not be moved.
    Thy keeper will never slumber.

  9. Ola Gjeilo (1978) - Northern lights

    Pulchra es, amica mea,
    Suavis et decora filia Jerusalem,
    Teribilis ut castrorum acnes ordinate,
    averte oculos tuos a me, quia ipsi me avolare fecerunt, amica mea.

    Thou art beautiful, o my love,
    sweet and comely as Jerusalem,
    terrible as an army set in array.
    Turn away thy eyes from me,
    for they have made me flee away.

  10. Arvo Part (1935) - Da Pacem

    Da pacem Domine
    in diebus nostris
    quia non est alius
    qui pugnet pro nobis
    nisi tu Deus noster.

    Give peace our Oh Lord,
    in our time
    because there is no one else
    who will fight for us
    if not You, our Lord.

  11. Henry Purcell (1659-1695) - My dearest my fairest

    My dearest, my fairest,
    I languish for you.
    Thy kindness has won me,
    Thy charm has undone me,
    I ne'er, no, ne'er shall be free.
    I faint with the pleasure I fain would repeat,
    Ah why are love's raptures so short and so sweet,
    Thus pressing and kissing, fresh joys we'll pursue
    And ever be happy and ever be true.
    But alas, should you change
    Ah, tell me not so
    No never my fairest
    No, no, my dearest (my fairest) no, no.

  12. Hans Leo Hassler (1564-1612) - Ach weh des Leiden

    Ach weh des Leiden,
    muss es dann sein gescheiden?
    Ach weh mir Armen,
    wen sollt's doch nicht erbarmen?
    Ach weh der Schmerzen,
    so ich empfind im Herzen.
    Muss ich dich dann aufgeben,
    so kost's mir mein Leben

    Alas, what sorrow I feel:
    must we part?
    Alas, poor me,
    who would not sympathise with my plight?
    Alas, what pain
    I feel in my heart.
    If I must give you up
    it will cost me my life.

  13. Josquin de Prez (c 1450-1521) - Mille regretz

    Mille regretz de vous abandonner
    Et d'eslonger vostre fache amoureuse,
    Jay si grand dueil et paine douloureuse,
    Quon me verra brief mes jours definer.

    A thousand regrets at deserting you
    and leaving behind your loving face,
    I feel so much sadness and such painful distress,
    that it seems to me my days will soon dwindle away.

  14. Pierre Passereau (1509-1547) - Il est bel et bon

    Il est bel et bon, commère, mon mari.
    Il estoit deux femmes toutes d'ung pays.
    Disanst l'une à l'aultre - "Avez bon mary?"
    Il ne me courrousse, ne me bat aussy.
    Il faict le mesnaige,
    Il donne aux poulailles,
    Et je prens mes plaisirs.
    Commère, c'est pour rire
    Quand les poulailles crient:
    Petite coquette (co co co co da), qu'esse-cy?

    “I tell you, girl, my husband is handsome and fine,"
    Once two women from the same village were gossiping,
    Saying one to the other, "Do you have a good husband?"
    "He doesn't scold me, or beat me either.
    He does the chores,
    He feeds the chickens
    And I take my pleasure.
    Girl, you have to laugh
    to hear the cries of the chickens,
    'Little flirt (co, co, co, co, da), what is that?'"

  15. Trad arr Eytan Pessen - Noches noches

    Nekton, noktoj, bonaj noktoj, noktoj por enamiĝ.
    Tiaj noktoj, patrin' mia, ne okazas iam ajn.
    Turniĝante en la lito, kiel fiŝo en la mar'.

    Nights, nights, good nights, night are to fall in love.
    Such nights, mother, that never arrive.
    Tossing and turning in my bed like a fish at sea.

  16. Trad arr David Conte - La martiniana

    Niña, cuando yo muera
    No llores sobre mi tumba,
    Cántame un lindo son, ay mama
    Cántame la sandunga.

    Lucero de la mañana,
    El rey de todos los sones,
    Canta la martiniana ay mamá!,
    Que alegren los corazones
    No me llores, no, no me llores, no,
    Porque si lloras yo peno,
    En cambio si tú me cantas
    Yo siempre vivo, yo nunca muero.
    Si quieres que te recuerde,
    Si quieres que no te olvide,
    Canta sones alegres , mamá!
    Música que no muere.

    Girl, when I die, don't cry over my tomb.
    Sing me a beautiful song, oh my love, sing me
    the Sandunga (a traditional Mexican waltz).

    Light of the morning,
    the king of all songs,
    sing the Martiniana oh my love,
    that that breaks hearts.

    Don't cry for me, no!
    Because if you cry, I suffer;
    instead, if you sing for me
    I live and never die.
    If you want me to remember you,
    if you want me to not forget you,
    sing melodies of the soul oh my love;
    music that never dies.

  17. Josef Rheinberger (1839-1901) - Abendlied

    Bleib bei uns, den es will Abend werden
    und der Tag hat sich geneiget.

    Bide with us, for evening shadows darken,
    and the day will soon be over.
